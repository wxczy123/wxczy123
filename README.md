# Chen Ziyue
﻿
I am currently a student at University of Chinese Academy of Sciences. My research interests include machine learning, large language models, particle physics, neutrino detection, and event classification.
﻿
My current research has two main components. One line of work focuses on machine-learning-based pulse shape discrimination (PSD) for liquid scintillator detectors. In particular, I am interested in representing detector events through time-charge (T-Q) information and using deep learning models to distinguish neutrino-like signals from radioactive background events.
﻿
Another line of work focuses on large language models, especially new fine-tuning methods and model adaptation strategies. I am interested in developing and analyzing fine-tuning approaches that can improve the efficiency, stability, and downstream performance of LLMs.
﻿
More broadly, my interests lie at the intersection of deep learning, scientific data analysis, and natural language processing.
